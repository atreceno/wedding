About Toledo

Toledo is set on a hill above the plains of Castilla-La Mancha in central Spain. It’s known for the medieval Arab, Jewish and Christian monuments in its walled old city, and as the former home of the artist El Greco. The Moorish Bisagra Gate and the Sol Gate, in Mudéjar style, open into the old quarter, where the Plaza de Zocodover is a lively central meeting place. How to get to Toledo

By plane

The closest airport is Adolfo Suárez Madrid-Barajas (MAD) airport. Once at the airport follow instructions on how to get to Toledo by any of the other means of transportation.

By train

From Terminal 4 of Adolfo Suárez Madrid-Barajas airport, take the Cercanías C-1 train line to Atocha. One single ticket costs €1.70 or you can buy 10 tickets for €10.00. In Atocha, head to the AVE section and take the high speed train to Toledo. It takes 33 minutes to get to Toledo and the return ticket costs €20.60. When the train arrives to its destination, take one of the buses that go to Plaza de Zocodover (lines 5, 5D, 61, 62, 94, 11) which should be at a walking distance to your lodging.

By cab

If you are not adventurous or don't feel like carrying your luggage up and down, you can book this taxi service that will take you from the airport to your lodging in Toledo. Just keep in mind you need to book it, at least, 16 hours before the transfer.

By car

If you are thinking of traveling around Spain, it may be worth to rent a car with a navigation system.
